Client Information for Software Development Capstone Projects

Dear potential client,
Welcome to the capstone undergraduate projects site. Central to this subject is that it gives our students experience in working in teams (of 4 to 7) to complete a realistic project over a semester for an authentic client.

Below is some background information about the projects. But the simplest thing is for you to contact me by email so we can discuss your potential project, discuss scope, determine which subject it best suits and to create a short description.

Professor Judy Kay
Judy.Kay@sydney.edu.au

What are the responsibilities of the client?

You create a brief project description (~100 words, plus links as needed). Students will use this to identify projects they want to nominate for. It should include information about special skills needed, constraints on IP and any requirement for work off-campus.

You can, optionally, attend the first lecture. That will give you 5 minutes to explain your project and to take questions about it. Soon after that class, students nominate their preferred projects. This, with other information, is used to form teams and allocate projects.

You should be available for a half-hour weekly meeting with the client-liaison team member plus answer email, aiming to do so within three work-days. For the first weeks, a little more time may be needed so that students can come to grips your project. Good feedback can make a huge difference to the quality of the overall outcomes. Students will work towards a series of small "releases" and they need client contact to inform and assess these. (Should you have unscheduled events that may make you unavailable, students will work around that.)

Clients do not grade the projects. But we will consult you for a frank assessment of your interactions with the group, their management of the process and whether the provided a product that meets your needs.

About the subjects

There are three project subjects. The two COMP subjects, are COMP3615 and COMP5615 Software Development Projects. These are a quarter of the student's load. The former is an undergraduate subject and the latter is part of the coursework postgraduate programmes. (The differences between these do not affect clients.) INFO3600 Major Development Project (Advanced) is half load and only available to students in Advanced degree programmes. There are different projects and requirements for COMP and INFO subjects.

Background on the projects

XP: This subject uses the Extreme Programming (XP) approach to software development. Many projects fit this well and are ideal for this subject; some are not. Our initial discussions will determine if your project is a good fit.

Teamwork: A core learning goal is to develop teamwork skills. So, projects must be suitable for groups, with tasks that can be split across the team and require an appropriate work load. This will also be part of our initial discussion.

Hosting and location: Students must use Bitbucket for our internal processes, supporting for teamwork and version management. It is also critical to the assessment processes. (Of course, clients can also ask for versions to run elsewhere.)

What can a Client Expect from the Projects?

Typically, students make a really serious effort to do a good job of what they perceive as their client needs. In general, clients can expect projects provide an excellent way to explore a new software need and gain a solid prototype. In many cases, our projects have gone into production use.

At the same time, clients must appreciate that the students are doing considerable learning, for example, about XP, team work processes and technical skills. They are also still quite inexperienced, yet to reach the peak level that calls for a few more years experience.
